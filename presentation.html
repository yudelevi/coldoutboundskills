The problem with cold email tooling

You need a dozen tools and a project manager's brain to run one campaign.

What you need

  • Domains + inboxes
  • A real ICP
  • Lead lists (multiple sources)
  • List qualification
  • Good copy
  • Deliverability monitoring
  • Reply measurement
  • A weekly rhythm

What you get from most tools

  • One of those, maybe two
  • A dashboard
  • An invoice

What makes it different

  1. One entry point. /cold-email-kickoff asks your infra status and orchestrates the first 3 skills.
  2. ICP qualification is required. Every list-building skill calls /icp-prompt-builder. No more wasted enrichment spend on bad fits.
  3. Copy has a schema. /campaign-copywriting emits variants.yaml. /smartlead-campaign-upload-public reads it. No copy-paste.
  4. Uploads are always DRAFT. The script never auto-activates. You review in the Smartlead UI and hit Start.
  5. Every skill knows the next. Each SKILL.md ends with "What to do next." No "I ran this, now what?"
  6. Weekly rhythm is part of the kit. The difference between hobbyist and top 1% isn't tooling — it's showing up on schedule.

One number separates a campaign worth running
from one that's burning your domains.

Overall reply rate, after 200 sends, must be ≥ 1%.

Below that: your copy, list, or deliverability is broken. The audit enforces it. The incident-response playbook triages it. The weekly rhythm catches it before it compounds.
